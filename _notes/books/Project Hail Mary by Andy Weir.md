### How the Read Went

Some books age poorly on a second read. But what surprised me here was that I went cover-to-cover with this one.

Weir writes with a science-nerd irreverence which gives a rhythm to what would have been a very long, very boring internal monologue. And the humor is not subtle at all, which does make you crack up at times.

### What Sits Beneath the Story

Yes, it is hard science fiction. Yes, it is built on improbable premises. But what sustains it isn’t the science; it’s projection.

You imagine Grace’s anxieties. You project personality onto Rocky (who is essentially a five-legged spider). The emotional investment comes less from what is described and more from what you fill in yourself.

Not many books give you the chance to enjoy their story as a teenager again like this one does.

### Should You Read It?

If you’re allergic to extended scientific problem-solving, then probably not. But it is a fun story otherwise.
